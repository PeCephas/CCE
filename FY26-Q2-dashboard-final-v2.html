Q2 FY26 (September - November 2025) | APAC & ME Division

Executive Overview

Highlights
  • CRM Operational Maturity: Successfully completed Beta email testing, implemented Braze governance framework, and delivered Phase 1 SFMC training across markets, enabling independent journey setup and publication.
  • Organic Growth Momentum: Delivered 2.05M SEO visits (+15% QoQ), 1,394 applications (+8% QoQ), and 409 first trades (+9% QoQ). Australia's CRO programme achieved 9.32% uplift in application completions with industry-leading 35% win-rate.
  • ASO Performance: iOS downloads grew 20% QoQ to 15,858 with conversion rates improving to 1.24% (+0.52pp), validating store listing optimization strategies despite 30% decline in visits.
  • Social Media Transformation: Post link clicks surged 131% to 501,522 through conversion-focused strategy. Engagement increased 170% to 756,940, achieving 166% higher engagement rates than competitors despite 95% smaller audiences.
Key Initiatives
  • IG Markets AU Crypto Rollout: Copy frameworks completed, wiki documentation under local review, Braze integration setup pending. CRM deliverables timeline being aligned with Product launch schedule.
  • IG SG CFD Triggered Communications Migration: Migration scope and timelines finalised with comprehensive QA protocols, segmentation issue escalation processes, and compliance frameworks established.
  • Project SWIFT: Global TechSEO remediation launched targeting 20-50% traffic uplift, addressing 60+ technical implementation issues with external developer support. Currently 30% complete with another 40% in progress. Internal support to complete tickets remains an issue.
  • AEM Global Access Audit: Complete overhaul of access entitlements, DAM configuration framework and training toolkit development finalised. Launch & release planned for 7 January 2026.
  • Braze Migration Readiness: Phase 1 market sessions completed and reviews in progress for SFMC communications and migration mapping. Comprehensive data validation and compliance verification planned for Jan 2026.

Executive Summary

Q2 demonstrated strong operational maturity across organic channels while exposing critical CRM performance gaps requiring immediate intervention.

CRM Performance Disparity: Triggered campaigns significantly outperformed manual sends across all markets. Japan Corporate achieved 72.81% open rates and 16.79% CTR, while manual campaigns remained critically low (0.01-0.92% CTR vs 4.9% fintech benchmark). This 5-48x performance gap validates triggered, behaviour-led communications as the primary engagement engine.

Regional Performance Dynamics: Japan led with 1.23M visits (60% of divisional traffic, +23% QoQ) and 173 first trades (-15% QoQ despite traffic growth), highlighting conversion efficiency challenges. UAE delivered exceptional 28% traffic growth to 379K visits with 35% improvement in first trades. Singapore sustained positive momentum with 34% first trade growth. Australia demonstrated conversion efficiency gains (+39% first trades) despite 7% traffic decline.

Strategic Weakness ‚Äì Manual Campaign CTR: Manual email CTR remains 12-490x below industry benchmarks across all markets, with November showing -26% MOM decline in applications and -29% decline in first trades, signalling potential seasonal vulnerability or market friction requiring Q3 investigation.

Competitive Context: APAC fintech market projected USD 304.6bn by 2030 (16% CAGR). Multi-asset, mobile-first brokers and white-label infrastructure providers increasing competitive pressure. Regional players strengthening through AI-driven compliance tools, localised funding rails, and Shariah-compliant products.

Immediate Actions Required: Launch structured A/B testing on manual campaigns to address stagnant CTR performance, complete Project SWIFT by December to unlock 20-50% traffic uplift, scale Australia's winning CRO components across regions, and implement frequency management to reduce elevated unsubscribe rates in AU EN and Dubai AR segments.

Highlights

APAC&ME Performance Analysis: APAC&ME demonstrated strong quarter-over-quarter momentum with 2.05 million total visits (+15% QoQ) and 1,394 applications (+8% QoQ) generating 409 first trades (+9% QoQ). Japan led regional expansion with 1.23 million visits, representing 60% of total organic acquisition and achieving 23% QoQ growth, while maintaining excellent search visibility.

Quarterly Market Performance Dynamics: UAE emerged as the growth standout with exceptional 28% QoQ traffic increases to 379k visits, coupled with a 35% improvement in first trade conversions to 35. Australia demonstrated conversion efficiency gains with first trades surging 39% QoQ to 142 despite a 7% traffic decline, signalling improved funnel performance. Singapore sustained positive conversion momentum with 34% QoQ growth in first trades to 59 and 25% growth in applications to 163, though traffic moderated by 9% to 119k visits.

Monthly Trend Analysis: November showed significant declines with divisional visits down 21% MoM to 639k, applications -26% MoM to 416, and first trades -29% MoM to 115. UAE (-53% to 8 FTs) and Japan (-36% to 42 FTs) experienced severe drops, while Australia demonstrated resilience with only a 14% decline to 49 FTs and Singapore fell 24% to 16 FTs. This broad-based deterioration across all markets suggests potential seasonal softness or market friction requiring close Q3 monitoring.

Content Strategy Evolution: Q2 delivered significant platform optimisation with the IG.com reskin project nearing completion across 70 brochure pages and 90 cluster pages in Australia, Singapore, and UAE, enhancing user experience and conversion pathways. The transition to localised freelancer and agency partnerships for semi-evergreen content maintained strong momentum, demonstrating improved content velocity with gradual month-over-month gains in traffic and conversions.

CRO Initiative: Completed a six-month conversion optimisation project with focus in addressing Australia's traffic-to-application conversion. Delivered 17 experiments with 6 winning tests achieving 9.32% uplift in applications (4,152 additional applications) and a 35% win-rate vs. 12% industry average. We're now building winning components into AEM for permanent implementation, with plans to scale successful optimisations to other regions via A/B testing.

Project SWIFT: Launched a global Technical SEO improvement project targeting 20-50% traffic uplift across all markets. Onboarded two external web developers and an SEO consultant to audit and address over 60 technical implementation issues, with the project currently 30% complete and requiring close coordination between technical SEO, internal development teams, and key stakeholders.

Competitor Analysis

Ranking Performance:

Regional search rankings remained stable throughout Q2, despite a temporary drop in September caused by Google's removal of the "#=100" parameter that impacted all major SEO tools' tracking methodologies. While Google's trust signals for these categories will take time to establish, early Singapore investment content is already gaining traction with improving rankings and conversions. Crypto CFD content is being published proactively ahead of the Crypto launch to build market presence and brand authority.
