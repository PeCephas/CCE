Executive Summary

Q1 demonstrated mixed results with strong conversion optimisation improvements across multiple channels. Despite a slight decline in total users (-3.2%), the we achieved a 6% increase in first trades (382 to 405), indicating improved funnel efficiency and better quality traffic conversion.

CRM communications showed significant operational maturity with successful completion of Beta email testing, implementation of Braze governance processes, and delivery of Phase 1 SFMC training across markets.

However, manual campaign performance remained critically low with click-through rates well below industry benchmarks (0.01-0.37% vs 4.9% fintech average), while triggered campaigns consistently outperformed with strong engagement rates, particularly in Australia's Simplified Chinese campaigns (6%+ CTR, 65%+ open rates).

Organic growth channels delivered solid traffic performance with 1.78M total SEO visits and strong app store optimisation results, including 447% year-over-year increase in App Store visits. IG Academy emerged as a key conversion driver with demo sign-ups surging 198%, though demo user retention challenges persisted across all markets, highlighting the need for improved user experience optimisation.

What These Benchmarks Suggest/Opportunities

  • Fintech open rates (≈27.1%) are significantly lower than the all-industry average (~42-43%) per MailerLite. This means: either fintech audiences are harder to engage, or there's room to improve subject lines, sender reputation, etc.
  • Click-through rates and CTOR in fintech appear relatively strong vs general averages, suggesting that once people open the emails, they are fairly engaged. That implies optimizing for open rate could yield strong marginal gains.
  • Unsubscribe rates remain low - this is good. Keeping content relevant and maintaining frequency could help keep this low and possibly reduce it further.

Opportunities for IG

  • Explore real-time data integration in triggered journeys (e.g. "Trade of the Month" live feed).
  • Scale personalisation at a segment level (e.g. different CTAs for high-value vs retail clients).
  • Apply A/B and multivariate testing consistently across all markets.
  • Develop re-engagement streams to address manual email fatigue.

Risks & Recommendations

Risks

  • Manual campaign CTR remains critically low across markets
    • AU EN manual: 0.01-0.31% CTR despite open rates ~46-53%.
    • Japan manual: 0.04-0.08% CTR (Corporate marginally higher at 0.10-0.39%).
    • Singapore manual: ~0.02-0.10% CTR vs fintech benchmark ~4.9%.
  • Triggered volume declining in Japan and Singapore
    • Japan triggered down from ~1.6M (June) → 1.2M (Aug).
    • Singapore triggered down from ~1.4M (June) → 0.8M (Aug).
    • → Indicates automation logic or entry conditions may be failing.
  • Unsubscribe and spam concerns in specific markets
    • AU EN manual unsub peaked at 0.31% in July.
    • Dubai AR triggered unsub rose to 0.33% in August.
    • Singapore spam complaints up to 0.048% in August, highest in region.
  • Onboarding journey drop-offs
    • AU Non-Leverage journeys: activations growing, but CIPC declining (£225 → £190), suggesting lower-value clients being converted.
    • JP App Incomplete journeys continue to underperform vs control (July 4.95% vs 7.69% in control).
    • Dubai onboarding conversion rates dropped sharply from June (35%) to August (15%).

Recommendations

  • Optimise manual campaigns
    • Introduce structured A/B testing for subject lines, CTAs, and layouts.
    • Segment more precisely by behaviour and lifecycle stage.
    • Separate mandatory vs promotional sends to avoid inflated opens with low clicks.
  • Stabilise triggered volumes
    • Conduct automation health checks in Japan and Singapore.
    • Validate trigger logic, entry rules, and audience eligibility.
    • Reinforce successful paths (JP Corporate, AU Simplified CN).
  • Address unsub and spam hotspots
    • Dubai AR: review localisation and frequency.
    • AU EN manual: refine content to reduce fatigue.
    • Singapore: reduce cadence, improve creative to lower spam triggers.
  • Strengthen onboarding ROI
    • Scale high-value paths (JP Pre-Fund & Normal Value Fund, AU Dual pathways).
    • Rework underperforming segments (JP App Incomplete, Dubai low conversion groups).
    • Track CIPC more closely to ensure quality, not just volume.
  • Benchmark-driven strategy
    • CTR across manual campaigns lags well below fintech average ~4.9% → immediate priority.
    • Triggered CTR (JP 1–2.4%, AU Simp 6%) already above benchmark → opportunity to scale globally.

Highlights

  • AU Simplified Chinese triggered sends deliver exceptional CTR (6%+), with open rates above 65%.
  • Triggered campaigns overall outperform manual, maintaining strong delivery (99%+) and engagement.
  • Manual English open rates are solid (46-53%), showing subject lines are drawing attention.

Areas for Improvement

  • Manual CTR is critically low (0.01-0.17%), indicating poor content or CTA effectiveness.
  • AU Traditional CTR remains <1%, limiting its contribution despite good delivery.
  • Some unsub growth (0.24-0.31% for AU EN manual) signals potential content fatigue.

Next Steps

  • Scale learnings from AU Simplified triggered success across broader segments.
  • Run structured A/B testing on manual content/CTAs to convert opens into clicks.
  • Separate operational vs promotional sends to improve accuracy of performance insights.
