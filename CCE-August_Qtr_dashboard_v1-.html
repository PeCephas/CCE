Executive Summary

Q1 FY26 Performance Overview: The APAC & ME region delivered strong results across all marketing channels, demonstrating the effectiveness of our integrated content and customer engagement strategy.

CRM Excellence: Our email marketing programmes maintained exceptional delivery performance at 98.4%, with 29.5M emails sent across the quarter. Triggered campaigns significantly outperformed manual sends, particularly in Japan and Australia markets. The key challenge remains improving manual campaign CTRs, which averaged below 0.2%.

Organic Growth Success: SEO traffic reached 1.78M visits with Japan dominating at 56% of total traffic. Singapore showed exceptional growth with 20% QoQ increase, whilst Australia and UAE faced conversion challenges despite stable traffic. The content production remained strong with 120 pieces delivered across all markets.

Social Media Transformation: Social channels demonstrated remarkable engagement growth with 207.7% increase in interactions and 262% boost in link clicks. The June-August period saw extraordinary platform-specific success, with UAE achieving 2,765.5% increase in link clicks and Japan showing 630% growth. The collaborative approach with local markets and platform-specific content strategy is yielding exceptional returns.

Education Platform Evolution: Q1 FY26's performance shows conversion optimisation improvement with first trades increasing 6% to 405 and FT CVR strengthening to 2.17%. Demo signups surged 198%, though demo user retention declined 21.2%. The key insight is that APAC has a strong "bottom of funnel" (demo to trade) but needs focus on "middle of funnel" (education to demo) conversion pathways.

Executive Summary

The key story from Q4 to Q1 data is conversion optimisation improvement. Despite having fewer total users, the region is generating more first trades through better funnel efficiency, particularly in demo sign-ups. The 198% increase in demo sign-ups addresses what was clearly a major bottleneck in the previous quarter. A strong demo-to-first-trade conversion supports that when users reach the demo stage, the experience is highly effective.

The main challenge is demo user retention - while more people are signing up for demos, fewer are sticking around, which is why the demo-to-first-trade conversion rate declined even though absolute numbers increased.

Key Performance Insights

  • First Trades Growth: +6.0% - Primary KPI improved from 382 to 405, First trade conversion rate strengthened from 1.98% to 2.17%
  • Demo Pathway: Demo sign-ups surged 198%, Applications doubled from 40 to 78 (+95%), Demo-to-first-trade conversions increased 19% (116 to 138)
  • Traffic Quality Improvements: Total users declined slightly (-3.2%) but prospect users remained stable (-2.2%), Sessions decreased (-5.5%) but conversion efficiency improved across key metrics
  • Demo User Retention Challenge: Demo users dropped 21.2%, Demo-to-first-trade conversion rate declined from 29.03% to 24.91%

Recommendations / Actions

  • Investigate demo: Why demo users are dropping off - fix the 21% decline
  • Optimise: Improve demo user experience to restore 29%+ conversion rates
  • Content: Create "what's next" pathways from popular educational content to demo trials (this is already in process for all new courses)
